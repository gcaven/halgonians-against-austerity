Invest in Transit

HRM Needs More Transit, Not Less

When HRM surveyed residents, the message was clear: we need more buses, not fewer.1 Some routes are seeing 45% ridership increases, and demand is only growing.2 Cutting transit in the name of affordability does the opposite: it forces families to spend outsized portions of their income on a car.

The Halifax Forum

Community Hubs Are Not Optional

The Halifax Forum has served the Peninsula's 100,000 residents for generations as a place to skate, gather, and connect.14 After four consecutive councils and more than a decade of planning, the reconstruction was finally moving forward. Now the Mayor wants to hit pause. Again.15

Defend the Forum →

Third Spaces Matter

Not every gathering place can be a coffee shop or a bar. The Forum is where kids learn to skate, where seniors stay active, and where community groups meet. It is imporatnt to the entire community of the North End.

A Decade of Planning

This project has been studied, debated, and approved by four consecutive councils.16 The design is done. The community has been consulted. Hitting pause now doesn't save money, it just restarts the clock.

The Building Is Failing

The Forum is nearly a century old.17 The roof is near collapse, there are serious building code issues, and the building lacks full sprinkler coverage.14 This isn't a renovation you can keep putting off.

ğŸ’¸

Delay Costs More

Construction costs in Nova Scotia are rising roughly 4% a year.18 The Forum's budget has already jumped from $81 million to $126 million.19 Every year of delay adds millions more. Waiting is the most expensive option on the table.

Road Safety

2025 Was the Deadliest Year on HRM Roads

Every death on a Halifax road is a policy decision.

Demand Safer Streets →
21

Deaths in 2025

The deadliest year in recent memory, up from 11 in 2024 and 6 in 2023.20 A 21-year-old Dalhousie student killed in a crosswalk.21 A 3-year-old struck on a residential street.22 These are not statistics. They are people who deserved safer streets.

0

The Vision Zero Target

HRM adopted a Vision Zero framework, aiming for zero fatalities by 2038.23 You don't get there by cutting the road safety budget. You get there by building protected crosswalks, separated bike lanes, and slower residential streets.

ğŸ“‰

Better Is Possible

Helsinki went a full year without a single traffic death.24 Hoboken hasn't had one in nine consecutive years.25 Oslo eliminated all pedestrian and cyclist fatalities in 2019.26 They did it with infrastructure, not luck.

🚧

Infrastructure Is the Answer

Enforcement campaigns come and go. Infrastructure is permanent. Protected crosswalks, roundabouts, raised intersections, and separated bike lanes save lives every single day without relying on anyone's good behaviour.

Protected Bike Lanes

Build the Bike Network. Finish What We Started.

Halifax promised a connected cycling grid by 2019, then pushed it to 2022, and now 2028. Every delay costs more money and more injuries: over 100 people were hurt while cycling in HRM in 2025, a significant increase from the year before.9

Demand A Completed Network →
ğŸš´

Separated Lanes Work

Building separated, protected bike lanes makes everyone safer: cyclists, pedestrians, and drivers. Traffic accidents dropped 25% after the South Park bike lanes were installed!10

Paint Is Not Protection

A 13-year study of 17,000 traffic fatalities found that painted bike lanes provide no measurable safety improvement. Only physically separated lanes reduce deaths and serious injuries. Cities with protected networks saw 44% fewer fatalities and 50% fewer serious injuries.11

Half Complete

The bike network is only 60% finished, currently made up of several disconnected pieces. Bike lanes that force people back into traffic don't inspire mode shift.

Better for Everyone

Every cyclist is one fewer car you're stuck behind. Streets designed for cycling reduce congestion for drivers and increase foot traffic at nearby shops.1213
